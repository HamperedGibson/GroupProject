Artificial Intelligence in Japan

Robots in the Tokyo 2020 Olympics

There are a variety of robots planned to be used in the Tokyo 2020 Olympics in order to aid disabled users. For example, the Human Support Robot (HSR) and Delivery Support Robot (DSR). These provide guidance to seats for the guests at the events and also transport around snacks, gifts, souvenirs and other light-weight cargo to the user's seat while they watch the games.

Artifcial Intelligence & the elderly

An aging society is a serious challenge for healthcare systems in Japan, with over 1/4 of the Japanese population being 65 or older. The Japanese healthcare insurance relies of data from more than 126 million people. This allows the AI to train on annonymous pieces of data with information such as blood pressure, body temperature, treatments and heartbeat.

Managing Japan's shrinking labour force

Japan's aging population and decreasing birth rate suggests shrinking in the labour force. While automation will eliminate very few occupations entirely in the coming decades, it is likely to have a huge impact on a variety of industries and professions. This allows Japan's economy to be protected from the threat of the shrinking labour fource, and keep production going.
